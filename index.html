Research

PhD

  • Subject: Designing explanation schemes for recommendations stemming from optimization systems,
    application to scheduling problems for facility management.
  • Abstract: Mathematical optimization offers handy tools for finding solutions to problems with well-defined objectives (e.g. scheduling). However, optimization systems (solvers or any software interfacing an optimization process) are often viewed as unexplainable black boxes whose solutions are inaccessible to users and with which they cannot interact. We propose to address the question of how to provide an output from an optimization system that is intelligible to a human, how to provide explanations in addition to the solutions obtained with an optimization system. This work is mostly in line with recent concerns about the construction of justification and explanation for recommendations in autonomous systems. With the recent era of explainable AI, models explanability and interpretability have gained significant interest.
  • Key-words: Operations Research, Scheduling, Mixed-Integer Linear Programming, Explanation.
  • Supervisors: Céline Gicquel (LRI), Vincent Mousseau (MICS), Wassila Ouerdane (MICS).
  • In collaboration with: DecisionBrain and IBM within the joint public-private (PSPC) project in AI called AIDA.
